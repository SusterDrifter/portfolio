TutorHelper

Schedule helper for tutors

Members: Andy Chan, Anthony Halim, Ooi Hui Ying, Ong Yan Zhi, Joel Lee

Work in progress

There is an extra requirement in designing TutorHelper UI which makes it differs from the rest. TutorHelper uses CLI (command line input) as its main form of user input, which implies that other forms of input such as mouse or touch screen should be minimised as far as possible.

The lack of mouse and touch interaction in the application restricts the usage of having separate tabs, dropdown, or similar kind of information container. In essence, this mandates that all informations must be displayed at once in a single screen.

  • v1.0

    The initial UI Mockup of the app. There is little information being shown as the app is still in its early stages and we do not know clearly what information to store. Intuitive and easy to understand, but everything appears to be on the same level due to similar size of text across the board.

  • v1.1

    The team roposed the idea of having a note, among other new features, for each students. Due to this, I removed Tutor's name, listing of the students, and checkboxes from the to-do-list from the screen. Additionally, the team requested a calendar view to make the date more dominant. However, the end product looks confusing and displeasing. The calendar view is supposed to represent all the students stored inside the app, not only representing Koki Lim's booking. However, the calendar being put under his name suggest otherwise.

  • v1.2

    A revision of v1.1. At this point, we decided to not use the note as there are more crucial that needs to be displayed. This layout better convey our intention, although some information that need to be displayed are still missing.

  • v1.3

    At this point, we have decided which information need to be shown on the UI. There is an overwhelmingly new information to be shown. In order to cater to this, I decided to throw away any organic shapes in favour of extra space, threw away the calendar view, while finding a way to make the timing of tuition still prominent. However, the lack of any fluid shapes makes for dull and uninteresting UI.

  • v1.4

    In order to counter the dull feeling of the UI, I decided to attempt to increase the saturation of each colour. I referenced Google website for the colour scheme.

  • v1.5

    For the last version, I decided to invert the colour in order to make it less blinding on the eyes.

Check out the project at github.

Health Interaction Application Heuristics

Setting a standard for health apps

Abstract

Health applications are slowly finding their own place in society. Being able to automate answers to some medical enquiry, these applications prove to be of great value and the dependencies on them are likely to increase over time. However, health applications pose a concealed risk as well. While these apps can be the starting point of a new lifestyle, wrong information by the health apps can lead to a disastrous outcome. Hence, this prompts the need for a good heuristics to evaluate the app.
